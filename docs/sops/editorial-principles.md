# Editorial Principles — the judgment layer (v0.1, 20/07/26)

*The canonical editorial brain for SW edits, from Joseph's original editor onboarding guide
(written for Mohammed). This is the "how to judge whether an edit is good" layer that sits
under every workflow. `reel-quality-bar.md` is the reel-specific checklist; THIS doc is the
reasoning behind it and the long-form equivalent. Items tagged 🤖 are things the AI pipeline's
selection / audit should apply.*

> **The one thing:** the goal is **NOT views — it's minutes of quality content consumed by the
> RIGHT people.** Kan plays the long game (a B2B buyer needs ~400 min of content before they're
> purchase-ready); every edit is one step in that journey. **Never sand off Kan's personality —
> authenticity IS the product.**

## Shared principles (apply to ALL content) 🤖

1. **Comprehension Decay.** The viewer starts at 100% comprehension at frame one; every word,
   cut, and visual either holds or drops that number. They exit when it drops below their
   threshold. **The edit's primary job is to slow the decay.** Every element must *earn* its
   place — distracting graphics, disconnected B-roll, and a loud music bed all cost comprehension.
2. **Misalignment Kills.** Every visual must *illustrate what's being said* or add clarifying
   context. Visually interesting but semantically disconnected B-roll = confusion = churn. (This
   is the principle behind the real review note "remove the B-roll, it pulls focus from the
   message" — b-roll that doesn't serve the words gets cut.)
3. **The Sacred Timeline.** Content that drifts from the video's promise gets cut. **Checkpoint:
   every ~30s the content should return to the core promise.** If the talent went on an on-brand
   but off-topic tangent, flag it to the lead (may become a separate clip) rather than silently
   cutting.
4. **Audio Priority Order** (the decision hierarchy when trading off): ① cut structure → ② audio
   quality → ③ pacing → ④ visual alignment → ⑤ motion graphics / lower thirds → ⑥ colour grade
   (least critical for educational content). *"A good idea with bad audio fails; a well-structured
   piece with mediocre graphics still works."*

## Hook architecture — short-form (first 2 seconds carry the most weight) 🤖

Three components must fire **simultaneously** in the first 2s:
1. **Visual** — the "stun gun": high motion, high colour, high contrast. Stops the scroll before
   the brain processes. Registered before text is read or audio heard.
2. **Title text (on-screen)** — words NOT being spoken; gives context + curiosity. **Separate
   from captions** (captions = the spoken words oscillating 1-2 at a time; title text = a larger,
   unspoken contextual layer). Matches REEL QUALITY RULES v2 #4.
3. **Spoken (first line)** — active voice, minimum words. KPI = **absorption rate**: if the
   viewer can't absorb the first line in one listen, it's too complex.

**Hook sequence:** visual stops scroll → first spoken line sets context → the **contrast** between
expectation and statement creates curiosity and locks the viewer.

**Direct vs implied contrast:** *Direct* ("most people think X — here's why that's wrong": state
the common belief, then flip it) is **stronger** than *implied* (a contrarian statement with the
belief left unstated). **Default to direct contrast** wherever possible.

## The 2 Dopamine Hit Rule (≤60s reels) 🤖
- Hit 1 = the hook. Hit 2 = usually **15-30s in**. Two hits before the natural exit = locked in;
  one = maybe; zero = exit before 15s. Tools: a sharp cut at the right moment, a confirming
  graphic, a reveal. **Identify both hits before submitting — if you can't, the reel isn't ready.**

## The two mandatory pre-submission tests (document both) 🤖
- **Eyes-Closed Test** — listen without watching. Bored = pacing too slow (tighten). Can't absorb
  = pacing too fast (add breathing room).
- **2x Speed Test** — watch at 2x. Can't follow = too dense OR captions unclear. (A growing share
  of viewers watch at 2x.) *Do NOT review short-form at speed during the lead's alignment passes —
  only as this pre-submission test.*

## The 7 Lego Bricks (consider ALL before submitting a reel) 🤖
1. **Topic** — can't fix a bad topic in the edit; flag vague/off-brand pre-cut.
2. **Angle** — the specific POV; the edit should make it land *harder*, not muddier.
3. **Hook structure** — the 3 components above.
4. **Story structure** — case study / listicle / narrative. Listicles move fast; narratives need
   breathing room. **Match pacing to structure.**
5. **Visual format** — split-screen / full A-roll / vlog POV. Establish before cutting; don't mix
   formats without reason.
6. **Key visuals** — B-roll/graphics must align with speech at each moment.
7. **Audio** — music sets the emotional register, SFX add emphasis; both support, never compete.

**Winning-clip framework (systematic hit-building):** when a reel over-performs, explode it into
the 7 bricks and write what each did; hold the best-performing bricks constant and improve the
others next time. Thin on data? Run the same exercise on the best creators in the niche. Build the
hit rate on purpose, not by guessing. (This is the Maddox loop / Hormozi "qualume" applied.)

## Mining reels from long-form 🤖
A good reel moment: **stands alone** (no context from the full video needed), has a **natural hook
built in**, has a **clear resolution** (ends on a landing, not mid-thought), and is **<90s raw**.
Process: watch once for content → watch again timestamping every shift/insight/story moment
(**4-6 per hour = healthy**) → for each, ask "could someone who's never seen Kan understand this?"
→ shortlist to the lead with reasoning; lead picks (brand priority + calendar) → cut approved
clips. **3 excellent reels/week beat 10 average.** Don't force clips that don't stand alone.

## Long-form (YouTube) specifics 🤖
- **A-Roll cut FIRST** (cameras only, no B-roll/graphics/music) → lead reviews structure → then
  full edit. A structural fix at A-roll = 5 min; at final cut = half a day.
- **Asymmetric pacing:** the **first point is the FASTEST** (30s-1min); points 2-4 get
  progressively longer (2-3-4 min). Creates the illusion of an accelerating video while it slows.
  Hit hard/fast first, then go deep. No padding into the opening.
- **Hook:** confirm the desire loop from the title/thumbnail and deliver the first dopamine hit
  **within 30-60s**. Exit before 60s = the intro failed. After 60s the title/thumbnail is
  forgotten; content takes over.
- Review V1 at normal speed; V2 onward at 1.5-2x.

## The note / annotation system (how feedback is structured) 🤖-audit
Three tiers — **MUST NEED** (non-negotiable, exact execution), **PREFERENCE** (suggested; pushback
allowed with reasoning), **OPEN TO** (full creative freedom). **Unlabeled = PREFERENCE.** Format:
`[type] – [what changes] – [why, tied to a principle]`; a note without a principle is only a
preference. The **50/50 ratio**: half the notes constructive, half positive — and **positive notes
are instructions to replicate**, not compliments. (For the AI audit: flag findings the same way —
tie each to a principle, and mark which are hard fails vs preferences.)

## Quality standards — when an edit succeeds
**Short-form:** hook delivers all 3 components in 2s · eyes-closed + 2x tests pass · 2 dopamine
hits before 30s · captions perfectly timed, 1-2 words oscillating · all 7 bricks considered before
submission. **Long-form:** retention holds the first 30% with no early drops · hook sound (context
+ desire loop + first hit ≤60s) · visual-word alignment clean throughout · asymmetric pacing
present · sacred timeline holds.
